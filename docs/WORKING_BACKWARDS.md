3.1 Vision - Long-term world you want PDE to create

Mission: PDE helps people make better decisions by understanding who they are.

Our goal is not to build another recommendation engine, another AI assistant or another collection of mini apps.

Our goal is to build a system that deeply understands each user and uses that knowledge to help them make better decisions throughout their daily life.

Modern life is full of choices.

Every day people spend time deciding what to watch, where to eat, what to read, where to travel, what to buy or which new hobby to start.

While there are thousands of applications that help users within a specific category, every one of them only understands a small part of the person behind the screen.

Streaming platforms understand movies.

Music platforms understand music.

Maps understand places.

E-commerce platforms understand purchases.

Social networks understand interactions.

Each platform optimises its own ecosystem.

None of them truly optimise for the person.

We believe people deserve something different.

We believe technology should understand the individual rather than the platform.

PDE exists to become that layer of understanding.

By building a continuously evolving model of each user, PDE aims to reduce the effort required to make decisions while increasing the probability that every recommendation genuinely matches the person's interests, preferences and context.

Our ambition is not to become another destination where users consume content.

Our ambition is to become the trusted decision layer that sits above every category and helps people spend less time searching and more time enjoying what they love.

3.2 The User Problem

Modern life offers more choices than ever before.

Every day people make dozens of decisions about what to watch, where to eat, what to buy, what to read, where to travel or what to learn.

Access to information is no longer the problem.

Finding the right option is.

While digital platforms have dramatically increased the amount of content available, they have also increased the effort required to evaluate it.

Users constantly switch between applications, search engines, reviews and recommendations trying to answer a simple question:

*"What should I choose?"*

The result is a repetitive decision process that consumes time, creates uncertainty and often ends with mediocre choices or no decision at all.

The problem is not a lack of options.

The problem is the growing cost of evaluating them.
